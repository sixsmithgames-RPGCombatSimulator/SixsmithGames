## Overview
ContentCraft keeps campaigns, novels, and connected settings consistent while you write, prep, and publish. It blends structured canon tracking with multi-stage AI workflows that respect your source of truth, so your world stays coherent across sessions and releases.

## Core Modes
- **Projects & Library**: Structured projects for campaigns, settings, articles, and supplements; shared canon library for NPCs, locations, items, encounters, and arcs.
- **Content Generator**: Stage-based AI workflows for NPCs, locations, items, encounters, story arcs, and writing (outline → draft → edit) with per-stage review and approvals.
- **Canon & Relationships**: Link entities, track references, and keep timelines and relationships synchronized across content.
- **Fact-check & audit trails**: Source-aware fact checking and version history so revisions stay traceable.

## Key Features
- **Canon-aware AI**: Draft, expand, and rewrite with models that read your saved canon; BYO API keys or use bundled credits.
- **Multi-provider support**: Run with Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google models (Bring Your Own Key) through a unified pipeline with consistent contracts.
- **Consistency & validation**: Each generation is schema-checked and reviewed before it joins the library to prevent contradictions.
- **Relationship mapping**: Characters, factions, locations, and items stay connected for quick recall and connected prompts.
- **Versioned content**: Iterations are tracked with history and diffs; restore prior versions anytime.
- **Prep-friendly exports**: Export structured outputs (Markdown/print-friendly) for tables, session packets, and writing drafts.

## Delivery & Access
- **Self-hosted**: Dockerized deploy with bundled MongoDB/SQLite; keep control of your data and access.
- **Workspace-ready**: Designed for teams or solo creators who need predictable, repeatable pipelines.

## Audience Notes
- GMs and writers who need a canon-safe assistant.
- Table organizers who prep multiple campaigns.
- Indie designers polishing settings and modules.
- Authors and bloggers keeping continuity tight across outlines, chapters, and revisions.
